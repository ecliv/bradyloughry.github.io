About

Bio:

Brady Loughry is a painter based in Knoxville, Tennessee, whose work focuses on architecture and landscape of the everyday environment. Working in an impressionistic style, he explores light, shadow, and color using these familiar places. He is currently completing his last semester at The University of Tennessee Knoxville, graduating with a BA in studio art and a minor in Pre-Med. He plans to pursue an MFA and continue developing work rooted in observation, place, and community.


Artist Statement:

Often I go on a run and take in the world as a blur. But within the blur I catch blips of the city that catch my eye. Small shadows, light, and color are most important to me. The descriptions of a place hidden in the details. When something catches my eye, I pull out my phone and snap a quick picture that is blurry, crooked, and imperfect. Despite these factors, the picture has everything I need. Back in the studio, I study these moments. I look at the way a shadow bends, the exact color it carries, how it relates to the surface around it. I like to take my time though, thinking through a particular place for weeks before I actually display it on canvas. Raw canvas allows me to be looser with the way that I paint, it also allows me to capture the essence of the moment. Acrylic spreads, skips, soaks, and drags across the rough texture working in its own way. My paintings come from my need to understand myself and the life that I am living. Breaking down moments and places into small paintings allows me to process where I am. When I paint, I think about where I have been and what it means to me. In the end, these paintings are my way of holding onto the parts of Knoxville that shape me. They are fragments of the city filtered through my own lens. By breaking the world down into small moments, I can understand it more clearly, and understand myself within it. I hope the viewer feels the same pause and shift in attention, and begins to notice the quiet details that make a place feel like home.
